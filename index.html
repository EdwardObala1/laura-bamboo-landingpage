TEKHAT

TEKHAT, The result of the need of affirmative action in the climate sector, is a venture in the region dedicated to the use of indigenous Kenyan bamboo to protect the local ecology, generate income for locals, and create products that are good alternatives to plastic and other destructive components.

Ambitions

Sustainable farming and product manufacturing is our business.
Sustainable growth and development is our ambition.

Communal Empowerment
We aim to improve local livelihoods and diversify the local communities using bamboo farming. Driving this as a means of propagating the growth of bamboo locally is a sure means to also develop local industries around bamboo farming, growing them to heights not achieved before.

Conserving Indegenious Bamboo
Creating sustainable bamboo products such as furniture, crafts, and construction materials to support local economies and reduce the reliance on harmful alternatives.

Environmental Conservation
Using bamboo for reforestation and soil conservation helps combat climate change, restore natural ecosystems, and provide a sustainable source of income for local communities.
